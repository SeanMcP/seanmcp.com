Over the past few months, I have grown frustrated with the organization of this
site. It contains a wide array of content, but all of it felt tacked on. I want
to write more, but the "system" was a demotivator. So I spent some time the past
few days reorganizing everything.

I've honed the concept of "Categories" and organized all of the existing content
into one of the four:

1. [Articles](/articles): Tech-focused guides, resources, and opinions
2. [Blog](/blog): General or personal ideas and posts
3. [Games](/games): Posts about board, card, and party games
4. [RPGs](/rpgs): Resources and thoughts about table-top role-playing games

Part of the reorgnization involed removing old categories. Articles absorbed
Resources. Blog spun off from Articles, "Web", and "Local". Games replaced
"Board Games", and RPGs replaced "Adventure Games"^[I still prefer this title,
but it wasn't common enough to justify].

Each Category now has a dedicated page that can be customized to meet its needs.
I'm particularly excited to build out the RPGs category with more content and a
tailored experience.

Part of the reorganization involved deleting old and low-quality Articles. I
don't think they will be missed, but there may be some hiccups in your RSS
reader.
